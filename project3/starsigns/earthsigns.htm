EARTH SIGNS

CAPRICORN / TAURUS / VIRGO

The earth element is all about imposing form, structure and foundation to projects, businesses and just about anything else that needs it, for earth signs build things. They are the producers of the zodiac, the ones that make things happen. Earth signs turn dreams into reality, whether their own or those of people around them. They understand all the steps one needs to take, from drawing up a budget, to scheduling deadlines, to overseeing the work flow to completion. Dedicated and responsible, you can count on an earth sign for good follow-through. Earth signs have a talent for harnessing available resources and making the most of them. A big part of their success is their "earthy" practicality. They are perceptive and realistic--you won't find any self-delusion here. Another earth sign asset is an uncannily precise sense of timing, a big advantage in any endeavor. They prefer to make long-range plans. Highly ambitious and goal-oriented, this group has a powerful will to succeed and usually do.

Earth signs are considered the builders of the zodiac. They often know what they want and do whatever it takes to get it. They see things in a tangible form, and they put a lot of emphasis on attaining the type of worldly possessions that they desire. They are loyal, patient and stable too, and their naturally cautious, conservative personality prevents them from rushing in and squandering time, money or labor. Earth signs are extremely down to earth and grounded. Another aspect of earth signs is their never-ending duty of responsibility. They take responsibility for all of their actions and sometimes for those around them. They are the most dependable signs of the zodiac and are always there to help a friend in need. Those who are earth signs can get caught up in the desire to obtain material possessions since they measure their success in these terms. Although they are reliable and trustworthy, they can also become materialistic. These signs need to find a balance between the two.
